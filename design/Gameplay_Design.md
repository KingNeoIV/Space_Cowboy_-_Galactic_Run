# SPACE COWBOY: GALACTIC RUN  
## Gameplay Design Document

---

## 1. Core Gameplay Loop

Space Cowboy: Galactic Run is a hybrid arcade action game that blends high‑speed space combat with fast, stylish on‑foot fighting. The player constantly shifts between flying, shooting, boarding, looting, and escaping.

### **Primary Loop**
1. **Travel through space** toward planets, sectors, and outlaw routes.  
2. **Engage enemy waves** in Galaga‑style shoot‑’em‑up combat.  
3. **Encounter motherships or bosses** that block your path.  
4. **Disable or defeat the mothership** in space combat.  
5. **Board the ship** and switch to side‑scrolling combat.  
6. **Fight through corridors**, defeat guards, drones, and elite units.  
7. **Kill or capture the captain** and loot the ship.  
8. **Escape before reinforcements arrive** and return to space.  
9. **Land on planets** to explore settlements, fight gangs, and gather resources.  

This loop repeats as the player travels deeper into the frontier, facing stronger factions and deadlier enemies.

---

## 2. Player Controls

### **Space Controls**
- **Move**: Horizontal and limited vertical movement  
- **Shoot**: Primary blaster  
- **Special Weapon** (planned): Bombs, charge shots, or outlaw tech  
- **Boost** (planned): Temporary speed burst  
- **Pause**: ESC opens the pause menu  

### **On‑Foot Controls**
Inspired by *Double Dragon* and *Strider*:

- **Move**: Left / Right  
- **Jump**  
- **Melee Attack**: Punches, kicks, combos, energy blade strikes  
- **Ranged Attack**: Plasma rifle, blaster shots  
- **Dodge / Slide** (planned)  
- **Interact**: Doors, loot, terminals, captains  

Movement should feel fast, responsive, and stylish.

---

## 3. Player Ship Design

### **Current State**
- Player ship moves and navigates space environments  
- Parallax planets and starfields create depth  

### **Planned Features**
- Primary blaster with fire‑rate balancing  
- Special outlaw weapons (spread shot, piercing rounds, EMP bursts)  
- Temporary power‑ups  
- Health or shield system  
- Visual feedback for damage  
- Ship upgrades earned through loot  

### **Design Philosophy**
The ship should feel like a **scrappy outlaw ride** — fast, deadly, and always on the edge of falling apart.

---

## 4. On‑Foot Player Design

### **Combat Style**
A mix of *Double Dragon* brawling and *Strider* acrobatics:

- Quick melee combos  
- Aerial attacks  
- Sliding strikes  
- Wall jumps (planned)  
- Ranged plasma shots  
- Heavy outlaw weapons (shotguns, energy cannons)  

### **Weapons**
- Plasma pistol (infinite ammo, low damage)  
- Blaster rifle (medium damage, limited ammo)  
- Energy blade (fast melee)  
- Heavy outlaw weapons (rare, high damage)  

### **Movement**
- Fast, fluid, and aggressive  
- Encourages rushing through ships and bases like a one‑man army  

---

## 5. Enemy Design

### **Space Enemies**
- **Bandit Drones**: Basic swarm enemies  
- **Syndicate Raiders**: Fast, aggressive fighters  
- **Remnant Drones**: Predictable but deadly patterns  
- **Elite Aces**: Mini‑bosses with unique attacks  
- **Boss Motherships**: Multi‑phase encounters  

### **On‑Foot Enemies**
- **Ship Guards**: Basic melee fighters  
- **Syndicate Gunners**: Ranged attackers  
- **Security Drones**: Hovering threats  
- **Heavy Enforcers**: Slow, powerful brutes  
- **Captain Bosses**: Unique moves, weapons, and arenas  

Enemies should be readable, fair, and fun to counter — but deadly in numbers.

- Boss mothership encounters

### **Mothership Levels**
Side‑scrolling interior maps:

- Corridors  
- Engine rooms  
- Cargo bays  
- Security checkpoints  
- Captain’s chamber  

### **Planet Levels**

- Syndicate bases  
- Raider camps

Each level type introduces new enemies, hazards, and pacing.

## 9. Difficulty Curve

### **Early Game**
- Simple enemy waves  
- Basic guards on motherships

### **Mid Game**
