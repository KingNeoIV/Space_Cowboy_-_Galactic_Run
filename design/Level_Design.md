# SPACE COWBOY: GALACTIC RUN  
## Level Design Document

---

# 1. Level Structure Overview

Space Cowboy: Galactic Run features a hybrid level structure that shifts between:

- **Space Combat Levels** (Galaga‑style)  
- **Mothership Boarding Levels** (Double Dragon / Strider‑style)  
- **Planet Exploration Levels** (side‑scrolling action)  

Each level type has its own pacing, hazards, enemies, and rewards. The game loops between these modes as the player
travels deeper into the Last Frontier.

---

# 2. Space Levels

Space levels are the backbone of the game. They represent the player’s journey between planets, sectors, and outlaw routes.

---

## 2.1 Space Level Features

- Infinite scrolling starfield  
- Multi‑layer parallax planets  
- Enemy waves and ambushes  
- Hazard zones (asteroids, debris fields, storms)  
- Boss mothership encounters  
- Loot drops from destroyed ships  
- Dynamic difficulty scaling  

---

## 2.2 Space Level Flow

1. **Enter a new sector**  
2. **Encounter enemy waves**  
3. **Fight through formations and hazards**  
4. **Trigger a boss mothership encounter**  
5. **Defeat or disable the mothership**  
6. **Board the ship** (transition to side‑scrolling)  

---

## 2.3 Space Hazards

### **Asteroid Fields**
- Randomized patterns  
- Breakable and unbreakable asteroids  
- High‑risk, high‑reward loot nodes  

### **Debris Clouds**
- Wreckage from old battles  
- Slows movement  
- Contains hidden loot  

### **Ion Storms**
- Distorts visuals  
- Disrupts blaster fire  
- Creates unpredictable enemy behavior  

---

# 3. Mothership Boarding Levels

After defeating a boss ship in space, the player can board it. These levels shift to a side‑scrolling action style
inspired by *Double Dragon* and *Strider*.

---

## 3.1 Mothership Interior Themes

Each faction has unique interior designs:

### **Iron Syndicate Ships**
- Industrial metal corridors  
- Red lighting  
- Heavy guards and gunners  
- Explosive hazards  

### **Raider Clan Ships**
- Chaotic layouts  
- Makeshift barricades  
- Fast melee enemies  
- Traps and ambushes  

### **Remnant Fleet Ships**
- Cold, sterile, automated  
- Laser grids  
- Security drones  
- AI‑controlled doors  

### **Star Marshal Carriers**
- Clean, organized  
- Shield barriers  
- Stun weapons  
- Tactical enemy formations  

---

## 3.2 Mothership Level Flow

1. **Boarding Cutscene**  
2. **Corridor Combat**  
3. **Security Checkpoint**  
4. **Drone Bay / Engine Room**  
5. **Captain’s Chamber (Boss Fight)**  
6. **Loot Vault**  
7. **Escape Sequence**  

Escape sequences may include:

- Timed explosions  
- Reinforcement waves  
- Closing blast doors  

---

## 3.3 Mothership Hazards

- Laser grids  
- Electrified floors  
- Turret traps  
- Conveyor belts  
- Explosive barrels  
- Security drones  

---

# 4. Planet Levels

Planets offer exploration, combat, and narrative flavor. Each planet is a side‑scrolling level with its own biome, enemies, and loot.

---

## 4.1 Planet Types

### **Frontier Towns**
- Settlements on the edge of civilization  
- Shops, NPCs, and optional quests (planned)  
- Local gangs and bounty hunters  

### **Syndicate Bases**
- High‑security compounds  
- Elite guards  
- Tech specialists  
- Heavy loot  

### **Raider Camps**
- Scrap‑metal fortresses  
- Fast, aggressive enemies  
- Explosives and traps  

### **Abandoned Colonies**
- Dark, eerie environments  
- Remnant drones  
- Environmental storytelling  

### **Ancient Ruins**
- Mysterious alien structures  
- Puzzle elements (planned)  
- Rare outlaw tech  

---

## 4.2 Planet Level Flow

1. **Landing Sequence**  
2. **Exploration / Combat**  
3. **Mini‑boss or faction leader**  
4. **Loot and rewards**  
5. **Return to ship**  

---

# 5. Boss Arenas

Boss fights occur in:

- Space (mothership battles)  
- Mothership interiors (captain fights)  
- Planet strongholds (warlords, AI cores, elite leaders)  

---

## 5.1 Boss Arena Design Principles

- Clear attack telegraphs  
- Environmental hazards  
- Multiple phases  
- Unique mechanics per faction  
- High‑reward loot drops  

---

## 5.2 Example Boss Arenas

### **Iron Syndicate Captain’s Chamber**
- Industrial metal platforms  
- Turrets on walls  
- Explosive barrels  

### **Raider Warlord Arena**
- Scrap‑metal throne room  
- Fire traps  
- Jumpable platforms  

### **Remnant AI Core Room**
- Floating platforms  
- Laser grids  
- Drone spawners  

---

# 6. Progression Structure

The game progresses through increasingly dangerous sectors:

### **Sector 1: Outer Frontier**
- Low‑tier enemies  
- Simple waves  
- Basic loot  

### **Sector 2: Syndicate Territory**
- Stronger ships  
- First elite captains  
- Planet raids become harder  

### **Sector 3: Raider Belt**
- Fast, chaotic enemies  
- Explosive hazards  
- Aggressive bosses  

### **Sector 4: Remnant Expanse**
- High‑damage drones  
- Complex patterns  
- AI‑controlled motherships  

### **Sector 5: The Dead Zone**
- Final region  
- Legendary captains  
- End‑game loot  
- Extreme difficulty  

---

# 7. Level Transitions

### **Space → Mothership**
Triggered by defeating a boss ship.

### **Mothership → Space**
Triggered by killing the captain and escaping.

### **Space → Planet**
Triggered by reaching a destination or discovering a landing zone.

### **Planet → Space**
Triggered by returning to the ship after completing objectives.

Transitions should feel seamless and cinematic.
