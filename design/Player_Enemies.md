# SPACE COWBOY: GALACTIC RUN  
## Player and Enemies Design Document

---

# 1. Player Overview

The player is **The Space Cowboy** — a lone outlaw who fights both in a starfighter and on foot. The design goal is to make the player feel fast, deadly, and stylish, whether weaving through enemy fire in space or cutting through guards inside a mothership.

---

## 1.1 Player Forms

### **A. Space Form — The Outlaw Starfighter**
Your ship is a scrappy, heavily‑modified outlaw craft built for speed and aggression.

**Capabilities**
- Fast horizontal movement  
- Limited vertical movement  
- Primary blaster  
- Special weapons (planned)  
- Dodge/boost (planned)  
- Health/shield system  
- Visual damage feedback  

**Design Goals**
- Feel responsive and precise  
- Encourage weaving, dodging, and aggressive play  
- Reflect the cowboy outlaw identity through visuals and upgrades  

---

### **B. On‑Foot Form — The Space Cowboy**
When boarding ships or landing on planets, gameplay shifts to a side‑scrolling action style inspired by *Double Dragon* and *Strider*.

**Capabilities**
- Light and heavy melee attacks  
- Ranged blaster shots  
- Aerial attacks  
- Slides, dodges, and acrobatics  
- Environmental interactions  
- Weapon pickups  

**Design Goals**
- Fast, fluid, stylish combat  
- Encourage aggressive forward momentum  
- Make the player feel like a one‑man army  

---

## 1.2 Player Weapons

### **Space Weapons**
- **Outlaw Blaster** — rapid‑fire, unlimited ammo  
- **Spread Shot** (planned)  
- **Piercing Beam** (planned)  
- **EMP Burst** (planned)  
- **Outlaw Bombs** (planned)  

### **On‑Foot Weapons**
- **Plasma Pistol** — infinite ammo, low damage  
- **Blaster Rifle** — medium damage, limited ammo  
- **Energy Blade** — fast melee, Strider‑style  
- **Heavy Outlaw Weapons** — shotguns, cannons, rare drops  
- **Throwable Tech** — grenades, EMP knives (planned)  

---

## 1.3 Player Progression (Planned)

- Ship upgrades  
- Weapon mods  
- New melee combos  
- Outlaw abilities  
- Faction reputation  
- Legendary outlaw gear

# 2. Enemy Overview

Enemies are divided into **space enemies**, **on‑foot enemies**, and **boss captains**. Each faction has its own style, behavior, and combat philosophy.

---

# 3. Space Enemies

These enemies appear during Galaga‑style space combat.

---

## 3.1 Basic Space Enemies

### **Bandit Drones**
- Cheap, fast, swarm‑style enemies  
- Attack in simple patterns  
- Die in one hit  
- Used by raider clans  

### **Syndicate Raiders**
- Aggressive fighters with better weapons  
- Dodge and weave  
- Fire bursts of blaster shots  
- Often appear in formations  

### **Remnant Drones**
- Old war machines  
- Predictable but deadly  
- Fire slow, powerful projectiles  
- Often guard derelict ships  

---

## 3.2 Elite Space Enemies

### **Interceptor Aces**
- Mini‑boss fighters  
- Unique attack patterns  
- High health  
- Drop rare loot  

### **Syndicate Gunships**
- Slow, heavily armored  
- Fire spread shots  
- Escort motherships  

### **Raider Bombers**
- Fly in straight lines  
- Drop explosive charges  
- Create hazards on screen  

---

## 3.3 Boss Motherships

Large, multi‑phase encounters that block your path.

**Features**
- Multiple turrets  
- Drone spawners  
- Weak points  
- Attack phases  
- Boarding opportunity after defeat  

Each mothership belongs to a faction and has a unique design.

---

# 4. On‑Foot Enemies

These enemies appear during boarding missions and planet exploration.

---

## 4.1 Basic Ground Enemies

### **Ship Guards**
- Standard melee fighters  
- Simple attack patterns  
- Low health  

### **Syndicate Gunners**
- Ranged blaster attacks  
- Take cover behind objects  
- Medium health  

### **Security Drones**
- Hovering enemies  
- Fire energy bolts  
- Explode on death  

---

## 4.2 Elite Ground Enemies

### **Heavy Enforcers**
- Slow, powerful brutes  
- High health  
- Charge attacks  
- Often guard captains  

### **Blade Assassins**
- Fast melee units  
- Dash attacks  
- Low health, high damage  

### **Tech Specialists**
- Deploy turrets  
- Summon drones  
- Hack doors or traps  

---

# 5. Boss Captains

Every mothership and major planet base has a **Captain Boss** — a unique enemy with personality, weapons, and a themed arena.

---

## 5.1 Captain Traits

- Unique intro animation  
- Distinct weapon style  
- Multiple attack phases  
- Special abilities  
- Drops rare loot  
- Affects faction reputation  

---

## 5.2 Example Captain Archetypes

### **Syndicate Captain “Iron Fang”**
- Heavy armor  
- Minigun‑style blaster  
- Summons guards  

### **Raider Warlord “Red Vulture”**
- Fast melee attacks  
- Jet‑boosted dashes  
- Uses explosives  

### **Remnant AI Core “Unit‑07”**
- Drone swarms  
- Laser grids  
- Teleporting attacks  

---

# 6. Faction Identity in Combat

Each faction fights differently:

### **Star Marshals**
- Law enforcement style  
- Stun weapons, shields  
- Defensive formations  

### **Iron Syndicate**
- Brutal, aggressive  
- Heavy weapons  
- Strong captains  

### **Raider Clans**
- Fast, chaotic  
- Swarm tactics  
- Explosives and ambushes  

### **Remnant Fleet**
- Cold, mechanical  
- Predictable patterns  
- High damage  

---

# 7. Enemy Progression

As the player travels deeper into the frontier:

- Enemies gain new patterns  
- Captains become more complex  
- Motherships gain more phases  
- Planet enemies become more dangerous  
- Loot becomes more valuable  

---
